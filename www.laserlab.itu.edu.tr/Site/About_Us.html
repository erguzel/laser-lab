Laser Research Group

 
 

About Us

 

The main purpose of our researches is to determine current problems pertaining the photonics science, and develop experimental approaches on novel optical materials which may generate potential solutions to those problems in long or short term.  We investigate the optimal conditions for fabricating such materials and synthesize them using different techniques.

Our focus is on the synthesis and optical characterization of glasses, bulk crystals and nano-materials doped with optically active ions. On this basis, we are using melt quenching and sol-gel methods, Bridgman Technique and some wet chemical techniques (such as thermal decomposition, combustion, sol-gel, Pechini method) to synthesize glasses, single crystals and nano-materials, respectively. For the optical characterization of the materials we are using the luminescence spectroscopy and we are able to do the response to pulsed excitation and luminescence spectrum measurements from 4 K to 300 K.
