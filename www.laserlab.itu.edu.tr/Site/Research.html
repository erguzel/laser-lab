Laser Research Group

 
 

Research

 

Our researches are centered on conducting experimental investigations of novel optical materials which have the potentiality of being used in Photonics Devices like laser amplifiers and sensor materials. For this purpose, we synthesize optical materials using different techniques and characterize both structurally (XRD, SEM, TEM) and optically (absorption, emission). The main tool for optical characterization that we use is Luminescence Spectroscopy which is very effective and important technique to investigate the interactions between light and matter.


Current Research Topics

  1. Incandescent lamp-like white light production from un-doped metal oxide nano-powders.

  1. Synthesis, structural and optical characterization of singly, doubly and triply doped doped Y2O3, YAG, GGG, Y2SiO5, Y2Si2O7  etc. nano-powders and transparent ceramics for novel sensing, illumination applications.

  1. Synthesis and characterization of singly, doubly and triply doped and/or modified tellurite-based glasses for sensing and/or laser amplifying applications.

  1. Designing lab-scale Optical Thermometers using Fluorescence Intensity Ratio (FIR) Technique.

  1. Novel type of single crystals via Bridgman growth technique.
